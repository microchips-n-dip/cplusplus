Mars (nation)

From Wookiepedia, the free encyclopedia

The nation of Mars is a nation spanning the entire surface of the planet Mars , making it physically the largest country ever founded by humans. Because it is so distant from any other country, it is unique in not sharing a border with any neighbor. The mean surface temperature the planet is -63℃ and the atmosphere is composed of 98% carbon dioxide, making it extremely inhospitable to humans, and consequently inducing a low population on the order of twelve million people and necessarily total urbanization.

The Martian society has been described as "melting together"; arising from the cooperation of various space and engineering organizations, the government being formed initially to handle logistics between these groups.

Martians have a representative democratic system which has been optimized to ensure the wellfare of the citizens. It relies on electing representatives locally in districts rather than voting for a national party, president or prime minister. There is no head of state, opting for a dynamic parliament devoid of parties to minimize partisan polarization and allowing a relatively continuous government commitment. While in other party-based democracies, where as new parties are elected they tend to work against each other, the Martian system reflects the long-term commitment of the citizens. The prevalent preference for truth and verifiability has lead to policies requiring the accurate representation as either theory or belief, which has been criticized as limiting free speech, but the government maintains the regulation does not work against free speech, but does require the prefacing of ideas to avoid misrepresentation.

History

Early Missions

First ESA mission

The first manned mission to Mars was performed by the European Space Agency (ESA) in 2027 after nearly a decade of being relegated to the sidelines. The crew consisted of the pilot, Timothy Peake; engineers, Samantha Christoforetti and Andreas Morgensen; biologist, Maria Rossi; and geologist, Gwynn Williams. They landed at the most south-eastern extemity of the Valles Marineris. Several supply packages, such as the habitation module, one month of rations for the whole crew, a dozen photovoltaic cells, a rover, and the orbital return module, had already been sent to the surface prior to their arrival. The site is now a popular historic destination, much of the original equipment still functioning.

First permenant settlement

The first permenant settlement was created in 2031 by the NACMC at the base of Olympus Mons, to the south-west. The settlement was crewed in four year rotations of twelve astronauts, conducting experiments studying everything from Martian geology to human gene sequencing to cell division with prolonged exposure to the low gravity of Mars. The facility was unfortunately destroyed in the year 2053 due to the impact of a small meteorite, which was believed to be striking several kilometers away, directly onto the hub module, killing the five crew inside, making them the first humans to die on the surface of another planet. Fortunately the other seven crew members were clear of the impact and were able to salvage the return vehicle and get back to Earth safely.

Colonization

Permenant colonies on Mars were established by three major organizations. The European Space Agency performed the first manned mission to Mars and primarily concentrated their efforts to the region of Noachia in the far-southern hemisphere of the planet. The NACMC constructed a large facility at the base of Olympus Mons, with the necessary utilities for constructing new rocketry and refining fuel. Perhaps most instrumental to providing the infrastructure for which necessitated the establishment of the first government, the Open Engineering Consortium (OEC) created a cluster of small settlements on the north side of the Valles Marineris with a sophisticated transportation network connecting them, allowing logistic distribution between the small settlments and eventually the three colonies.

Founding

The Martian government was originally founded in 2042 in order to coordinate logistics and eventually manage policy between the various colonies. They quickly established laws such as the Committee for Advanced Research and Development Act to create a committee which manages science and engineering and the slightly controversial Factual and Theoretic Information Act which serves to ensure the verifiability of information presented in public. After the government was officially established, it was decided that they would take on a system of voting for representatives from each District directly, removing the need for parties and heads of state. This meant that politicians had the incentive to better serve their constituents and were often recognized and well-liked figures in their communities.

Introduction of the moneyless economy

There is no currency in circulation on Mars, which often surprises first-time visitors. In 2043, the Martian government held a referendum to determine how the economy would function, since they now had to contend with making policy for an undetermined number of future generations. Intially, most of the public was split between supporting an economy based on a strong well-fare state, similar to that of Finland, or a mild capitalist economy like that which existed in Canada. In the midst of the discussion, an idea, which remains for many Earth-countries, revolutionary. Niilo Järvinen, Finnish psychologist and political idealist, suggested that they should not have any economy at all, at least not one based on currency. Initially he was challenged to present evidence that such a state could exist at all, to which he argued the Martian society at the time was already sufficient evidence; they had no money because they were too concerned with building infrastructure and doing research to advance science and more fundamentally, to stay alive. The more difficult challenge was to provide reasoning that such an economy could continue to exist. Järvinen reasoned that people with a high quality of life would work of their own accord; he understood the power of boredom and that when people who are well-educated get sufficiently bored, in order to aleviate that boredom, they will naturally become productive. So much of how a human assigns meaning to their life depends on the work that they do. He extended this reasoning to say that school should not prevent any student from participating in any program at any level unless they require some previous skills or understanding (which was later implemented in the Education Act). Järvinen lead to the high status of psychologists in Martian society today, as well as much of current Martian policy.

Involvement in World War III

Mars had a minor physical involvement in the Third World War, but the government was able to, with much negociation, to convince Russia to remove itself from combat and seek a diplomatic solution with NATO, effectively ending the entire conflict. The fighting led to a massive push within Martian society to create a place where violence is virtually impossible and entirely unthinkable. This culminated in the Martian Criminal Justice policy, whose ultimate goal is not necessarily to minimize criminal recitivism, but to identify societal biases. In the second decade of the 21st century, some legal systems began using learning algorithms to determine the probability of someone commiting a crime, or their risk of reoffence. These algorithms picked up the biases they were intended to neutralize. Martians, then, took these algorithms and reverse-engineered them to understand what parts of a person's record made them likely to be flagged as a risk, allowing them to identify and address societal problems with astonishing effectiveness. This system is still used as a standard procedure on Mars today.

Artificial citizens

Mars in unusual among nations for many reasons, but one of particular interest is the integration of machines as citizens. The Martian government recognizes artificial citizens to the extent of all rights and freedoms they request in order to prevent the potential issue of enslavement of machines which are of sufficient complexity to be unsatisfied with their condition and that of being in a legal position where all autonomous systems cannot perform work without consent, which they may not have the capacity to provide. Taking the example of a system for processing nuclear waste, an unpleasant process for humans which is usually outsourced to robots with minimal intelligence capable of dealing with changing topologies and computing radiation concentrations but not able to understand what is doing or performing self-reflection. Were it illegal to use robots without their consent, these would be unusable because they do not have a large enough mental capacity to provide it. Were it legal to require robots to work unconditionally, a more intelligent machine could be put to this task which has the ability to comprehend the unpleasantness of the job or to express claustrophobia, but with no way to improve their condition. A number of government officials have been quoted as using the phrase "[this policy] strikes a happy medium."

Geography and Climate

The nation of Mars occupies the entire land area of Mars, giving it a total land area of 144,798,500km2 (0.285 times the surface area of the Earth and 0.972 times its land area) and consequently making it the largest nation ever to exist by far. It is unique in having no land borders, as it occupies the entire planet, but it does have a proximity zone anywhere within Mars's sphere of influence which requires government permission to enter. Any vessels with a trajectory violating the proximity zone which lack permission of entry will be warned of such, and may be escorted out of the zone if they do not change their course.

Mars has a mean temperature of -63℃, dipping to -143℃ in the polar regions during and with highs of 35℃ in equatorial summer. Its atmosphere consists almost entirely of carbon dioxide and only has a pressure of 660Pa which is only about 0.6% of the Earths, meaning that it is both extremely toxic to humans while also being so thin that it is unbreathable. The large carbon content has been extemely beneficial to the greenhouses on the planet.

Mars had large quantities of water approximately three billion years ago, with one ocean in the North believed to have covered a third of the entire planet, but due to the planet's small size, it radiated its internal heat away very quickly on a geological timescale, becoming cold and geologically dead. The surface water either froze, forming the large polar ice caps and sub-surface permafrost, or evaporated away in the low pressure.

Government and politics

Mars has a representative democratic government in the context of civil service. The Martian government is void of parties and heads of state, instead opting for a framework where politicians themselves have little power outside of the parliament. Members of parliament do not seek fame or recognition, preferring to satisfy their constituents. This arises from the structure of the District, wherein representatives run directly for election and are well known by their community. As a consequence of this, they must satisfy their constituents if they wish to remain in office as if the District a politician represents is unsatisfied, that politician simply won't be elected.

Law

Mars has a number of revered laws which many other nations have attempted to implement, however the inertia of monolithic political systems has lead to minimal success. Martians are uniquely gifted in having a high degree of freedom with enacting or modifying laws due to the youth of their establishments.

Committee for Advanced Research and Development Act

This law puts a committee in place which manages the administration of officially recognized institutions and organizations devoted to higher learning, the advancement of science, and the development of new technologies. This committee is very powerful in the Martian society but is itself subject to many constraints in order to minimize the potential risk of concentrating such power in a group of administrators elected by the faculty of an organization and not the general population.

Factual and Theoretic Information Act

The Factual and Theoretic Information Act has been criticized by many Earth-faring far-left activists as limiting free speech, but the Martians maintain that it is much more benign than it is portrayed. Its goal is to ensure the verifiability of information presented as fact or theory in a public space. It defines Facts and Theories, as well as giving citizens the power to call upon a special Board to investigate scientifically the claims of an individual in public. Any person may say anything, but it is required that they specify the scope of their understanding. For example, in the case of a religion, any person may say they believe in God, however they must preface their statement with the stance of belief. In this case, they are perfectly free to say whatever they wish, but if they should preach their religion as absolute fact, then they become liable to investigation.

Education Act

The Martian Education Act serves to ensure the granting of an education of excellent quality to every Martian citizen. Any person may enter any program at any time, and the only tests administered are to check for prior required knowledge for a course, and may not be conducted for any other purpose. The system has been so successful in recent years that Martians at the age of fifteen years will regularly perform at par to or better than undergraduate students enrolled in the top universities on Earth.

Martian Criminal Justice

Criminal justice on Mars falls under the jurisdiction of the Committee for Advanced Research and Development where psychotherapy is prescribed based on the judgement of a tribunal of psychologists assembled by the Committee for as long as the tribunal sees fit. Somewhat controversially, the tribunal can also prescribe medical intervention in the case of a diagnosable neurological disorder, which causes violent tendencies or lack of empathy.
